User Evaluation of Rock Climbers Using 3D Digital Topographies
REB25-1062

Participation is voluntary and anonymous. The University of Calgary Conjoint Faculties Research Ethics Board has approved this study.

Purpose of The Study
The purpose of this study is to test a new 3D digital guide for rock climbing routes. We want to understand whether this type of tool can help climbers plan, prepare, and execute on their climbs, and how it compares to the guidebooks, photos, or apps that climbers already use. Your feedback will help us improve the tool and learn more about how technology might support climbers in the future.
What Will I Be Asked To Do?
You will be given access to a mobile app which has been designed to support you on the specific climbing route you mentioned in the initial intake form which you responded to. You can use it during your usual planning or at points on the climb where you would normally look at a guidebook or other climbing information. We will not be present during your climb. After you have had the chance to use the app, we will ask you to contact us. We will then invite you to complete a short questionnaire (with questions like “How helpful did you find the app for planning your climb?”) and may ask you to take part in a short interview (about 20–30 minutes) where you can share your thoughts about your experience. We may also ask you to use the app during this interview so we can see how you preferred to use it, and if you used it in the ways we anticipated. The interview may be audio-recorded so we can accurately capture your feedback; the recording will be transcribed, de-identified, and then securely deleted. 1 All participation is completely voluntary. You may choose not to participate at all, skip any part of the study, decline to answer any questions, or stop participating at any time. If you choose to stop, there will be no penalty or loss of benefits — for example, your access to climbing resources or other services will not be affected.
Are there Risks or Benefits if I Participate?
There are no additional risks to you from participating in this study beyond those that are already part of normal rock climbing. You will be climbing routes that you have chosen yourself, at times and places of your choosing. The 3D application is designed to be used as a planning and navigation tool, similar to guidebooks or other climbing apps. You will be advised to use it only when and where you would normally consult those types of resources. Possible benefits of participation include the opportunity to try out a new climbing tool that might help you visualize and plan routes, as well as the chance to contribute feedback that could help improve this type of technology for the climbing community. However, these benefits cannot be guaranteed.
What Happens to the Information I Provide?
Only the researchers involved in this study and their supervisor will have access to the information you provide. This includes responses to questionnaires and any recordings or notes from interviews. Your name will not appear on any of the data. Instead, we will use a code (or your chosen pseudonym) to keep your identity separate from your responses. Any audio recordings from interviews will be transcribed, with all identifying information removed, and the recordings will then be securely deleted. 2 The information you provide will be treated confidentially. Any results we share in presentations or publications will only include summarized, anonymous information, so that no individual can be identified. You are free to withdraw from the study at any time until we publish the paper containing the anonymous data. If you choose to withdraw before that point (approximately 1 month from the data collection period), all data you provided will be securely destroyed. Once the anonymous data has been included in a publication, it will no longer be possible to remove individual data because it will not be linked to your identity.
